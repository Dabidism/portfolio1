Rationale

Nowadays, university gate entry and exit systems have a huge role in premises security, traditional methods like ID checks or manual logbook records are often used to entry access university gate systems. However These methods can cause human error, without a real-time system, security personnel may have difficulty monitoring incoming vehicles which could increase the risk of security violations.

The proposed Smart Vehicle Gate Pass System, which integrates Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) and Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) technologies, aims to address these issues by providing a smart, web-based platform for the university premises. Each registered vehicle has a unique RFID tag that instantly detects as it approaches the campus gate. While this happens, a high-resolution camera captures the license plate, to verify the plate number registered based on the database record. By combining an RFID tag and ANPR it cross-match the recorded data based on the registered vehicles, the system can reduce the risk of tag-cloning, transferring to another vehicle, or license plate forgery. It also automates the process of logging each vehicle's entry and exit, including vehicle information, timestamps, and parking status, without the need for manual input. This dual-verification method improves the accuracy of vehicle identification while reducing the chances of human error. Additionally, the automated data collection and verification helps lessen the administrative workload, enhances response times, and streamlines overall gate operations.

This system is based on contemporary studies and real-world implementations of vehicle access systems across educational establishments. It responds to the prominent gap in campus-level adoption of integrated, technological-driven gate pass systems. With features such as real-time vehicle tracking, online registration approval, parking availability monitoring, vehicle logging, and data analytics records, the proposed system aligns with the global smart campus system.

Eventually, the objective of this study lies in its contribution to campus safety, working efficiency, and digital modification. Introducing a model that is both versatile and expandable. Moreover not only supports Iloilo Science and Technology University (ISAT-U) in its purpose but provides its technological innovation and a helpful consideration for other institutions aiming to modernize their campus security system.
